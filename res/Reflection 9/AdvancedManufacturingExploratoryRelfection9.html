        Throughout the “Dice” project, there were many skills I needed to learn, many safety hazards to be aware of, and many activities to enjoy. Two of the skills that I have learned throughout this project are to properly measure the faces and to learn to use the mill. One of the tools used to measure the faces was the dial caliper. The dial caliper can precisely measure with tolerances of ±0.001. When cutting down the faces of the aluminum dice, they needed to be 1.9 inches with a tolerance of ±0.01. The lowest measurement of the face is 0.89 inches, and the highest measurement is 0.91. To read the dial caliper, the caliper goes in between the two faces. The reading on the ruler determines the first two digits, while the reading on the knob determines the last two. Combining the readings will result in the final measurement. Another skill that I learned was to use the mill. Before I use the mill, I need to first set it up. To set up the mill, some of the things I needed to consider was to make sure the speed is correct, make sure the tool and tool handle is in the spindle, to make sure the machine is in the right gear, and to make sure the part is in the vice. The part is held on parallels to make sure the part stays straight in the vice. When the part doesn’t stay on the parallels, a plastic hammer is used to force the part on the parallels. The collet or drill chuck goes in with the tool. The tool goes inside the collet or drill chuck. For dynamically-sized tools that cut on the Z axis, the drill chuck is used. For other tools, the collet is used. To load the tool into the machine, the spindle needs to be positioned at the top and the “IN” button needs to be pressed. The machine will load the drill chuck or collet into the machine until the hammering sound is heard. Once in, the machine can be used. Once the machine is on, the speed needs to be set. The speed can not be set when the machine is off. Some tools need to be in a precise location on the part. On the X and Y axes, the edgefinders are used to accurately locate an edge on the part. Edgefinding more than one edge can find the center position. On the Z axis, the top is found by Z-Zeroing the part, which involves moving the spindle down until it hits the part. To cut the dice’s faces, the face mill was used. A drill bit was used to machine the pips of the dice. The chamfers used a chamfering tool. When cutting, chips from the part are going to excel outwards. A shield is recommended to prevent most of the chips from hitting the person that is operating the machine. When turning off the machine, the break is used to stop the spindle. The break should not be pulled when the machine is on.

        Two important safety hazards to be mindful of while using the machines are getting caught in the machine and the risk of the machine causing smoke or fire. A safety measure used is to not wear anything that will get caught in the machine. Wearing wristbands, jewelry, and other accessories can be fashionable, but can be very dangerous in an area with many machines. Another important factor is to make sure long hair does not get caught. Long hair needs to be pulled back so the machine will not grab onto it. If it gets caught in the machine, everything else will go with it, presenting a major safety issue. To prevent something from getting into your eyes, safety glasses need to be worn. Shirts need to be tucked in so the machines can not grab onto it. Another important safety hazard to be aware of is the possibility of the machine having an issue and causing smoke or fire. A safety measure to prevent this is to make sure to use the machine properly and use the emergency stop button. The emergency stop button, on both the bandsaw and the mill, is available to immediately stop the machine if something goes wrong.

        Two examples of what I enjoyed doing during the “Dice” project are using the mill and learning new tools. Using the mill was enjoyable because there was a lot involved in doing it correctly. Learning the steps and applying them to get a final result is enjoyable for me. My favorite part of using the mill was machining the pips. They involved edgefinding the sides of the die and finding the center so the pips will be in the correct position. Finding the center required a lot of math and was enjoyable. Aligning the Z axis wasn’t easy, but an enjoyable challenge. Machining the pips was satisfying to watch. Learning new tools, such as the dial caliper, helped me throughout the process. Using the dial caliper wasn’t easy, but I can precisely measure things to tolerance. Another tool used in the process was the files. The files helped file down the dice to make the edges less rough. This prevented the parallels from being at an angle. I believe this exploratory is one of the exploratories that is a good fit for me. I learned new things in this exploratory, like how to read a blueprint, ruler, and dial caliper. This exploratory is a new experience for me and it uses my strengths in regions like mathematics, physics, and knowledge of materials.
